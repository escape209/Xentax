@shekofte:
According to your untidy chart it seems to me that you don't understand the general structure of a todays computer which hasn't been changed much in general for more than 60 years. ("von Neumann"-structure is described here (German)).

It makes no sense taking a RAM-snapshot to analyze algorithms or data structures, because if data is loaded into the RAM, it is likely that the software modifies and stores it in a way where it can it process easier.

If you want to learn some hacking, you could get some old games for Windows-95 (which have no sophisticated CD-protections in most cases), a decent disassembler and a hex editor, and try to remove CD-requests.
My first Windows-target was Tomb Raider 2. It was easier than I thought...
