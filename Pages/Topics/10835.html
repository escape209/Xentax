Post by Mr.Mouse » Sat Oct 05, 2013 9:32 am

Here's another, be it more civilized letter:
Hello - I am posting this to ask if I can get an account on Xentax. I want an account simply to contribute to the research done on GTA V (for the Xbox specifically), but cannot due to the fact that I cannot register an account without donating.
I can assure you that I will not pay for an account to contribute to a forum that shows great interest in open game modifications and research. The thread I am specifically refering to is this one, and I find it quite ridiculous that many are complaining about software developers releasing only screenshots of GTA V modification programs, but at the same time - you guys are charging to contribute to the free and open research of GTA.
Now, don't take this the wrong way, I just said what I think is true. I understand that you guys have bills to meet, and I'm sure that's why you are charging to register an account. I am just hinting you on to reconsider, with the fact in mind - contributing to a thread that believes in free, open source research should additionally be free, and charging to help your forum just seems crazy. Free, open source, and public research is the best - and this problem seems to be reducing the amount of information available to the public (because I'm sure I'm not the only one who'd like to contribute and help).
Again, don't take this the wrong way - I'm just suggesting here. If I didn't care, I wouldn't have taken the time to write this email. I am just concerned because the thread has great information (the best anywhere on the web), but I cannot contribute.
And my reply:
Your points are understood. Still, this forum has been around for 10 years, and only this year we had to start asking for donations, since Google Ads and Google continously start to protest at our content, primed so by disgruntled users. We had to find other means to keep it up. Also, it's nothing like a subscription, as it is one time only, for a life time of access. Additionally, the 5 euro donation is the lowest possible. If you can get into the forum for 1 year or more, based on your own interest, there after, you can calculate the actual cost per month (40 cents, for two years 20 cents, for three years 10 cents etc.). You cannot call that having to pay any substantial amount that warrants your purist (and archaic, if I may add) stand on "open information". Besides, the forum text is completely open for anyone to read. An added benefit of this method of forum entry is that spammers and fools are not registering, it keeps the forum clean of people that just want a quick-fix, and brings in the more serious users. Nevertheless, if anyone can show us that they mean well and have stuff to offer to contribute and promote our community via one or the other, they get registration without having to donate a single buck. We've added people this way in the past, so there. Anything is possible.
