It depends on how you will design you program, example, 3d ripper dx, it hooks on the game's exe to access its video renderer, specifically DirectX9/8 then, captures data from the render data, decodes it then recodes it back as a readable data, to be exported for your program. mainly all known programs that firsak linked functions just like that. except for 3dvia prinstcreen.

the downside of this method, is you have no bone data for rig and animation, you'll only get meshes and textures.
go here for more info on 3dripperdx > http://www.deep-shadows.com/hax/forum2/

Post by drunkenchipmunk » Tue Nov 23, 2010 10:41 am

Chakuta wrote:Thank omfgpota
but i want to make a psp model ripper . Because there is already a model pc ripper, and my program would have no chance
3D model rippers use the "man in the middle" hacking technique to read the 3D models.

in the case of a PC, most games use DirectX or OpenGL to render graphics. What these programs do is to fully replace the DirectX driver with a custom one, that ends calling the native driver, but also logs the render calls to a file, which is later interpreted.

The bad news for you is that you're not going to find what you want, I mean: a solution that can be explained in detail in just two lines. You need to have Guru level knowledge of the driver from which you want to get the models.

I don't know about the PSP, but I think in most consoles, including PSP, there's no "graphics driver", the game communicates directly with the hardware, so the "man in the middle" attack is simply not possible. In that case, you're out of luck, and the only way to get the models is the old way, by reverse engineering the file format.

Another option for PSP would be to develop a full blown emulator (which is also a huge task), but, from my knowledge, PSP hardware is very close to the one of the PS2, and the PS2 sends the 3D models already projected to the graphics pipeline, so you would capture the 3D models with a lot of distortion.

If you have understood half of what I've said, you'll understand how difficult is what you're trying to do.... so, don't ask again "but how I do it"
