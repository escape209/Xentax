Good day!

It is my pleasure that you can come to read this letter,even if you are very busy.Thank you!

We are the one of Chinese game dubbing groups. Although a non-profit organization, but we also have some professional voice actors here. We all love dubbing and our accents range from all across china's vast expanse. Once I met Assassin's Creed, I was absolutely riveted by the story of it. So special, so exquisite. What an amazing game! Exactly, I make a decision at once! Therefore, I organized a group of dubbing speicalist who love Assassin's Creed game highly. We decided to put the Assassin's Creed into Chinese dubbing! But I need some more help and support in addition to my friends, that’s why I write to you, to your group. Will you please give me some help? For that I am very grateful! God bless you and your group!

By the way, the following tools is what I need:

1.PCK in the audio file import and export tools;

2.Unpack and repack tools of “DataPC_StreamedSounds.forge” in Assassin's Creed I（audio file ）

3.Unpack and repack tools of “DataPC_StreamedSoundseng.forge” in Assassin's Creed I（audio file ）

4.Import and export tools for the text of the dialogue

5.encrypt and decrypt for the text of the dialogue



Caused by stiffer nationaI policy and censorshipp, little games (original edition) can be imported in China, for example: Assassin's Creed III. But these still can't stop my passion for the game! English Assassin's Creed will be in Chinese! I spent five hours to finish writing this letter, because fans are waiting for my efforts and your help!
So,XeNTaX community? Can we help out here?

It was never my intention to replace/re-pack the forge files or provide a tool for any kind of localization. Not sure if I have ever stated that publicly in the forums, but I have said that to everyone who has ever asked.

It has always been my intention to provide a visual way of extracting game assets (models, textures, etc.). For that ForgeX and its successor Archive X does what it is intended to do. And as soon as I can wrap my head around the way WPF works with multiple meshes, model viewing will be working in Archive X.

Back to the subject at hand.

I was able to find string files in Prince of Persia. They were simple enough, not encrypted, easy to read. I found the same file designation in Assassin's Creed, however, those files appear to be encrypted. Everything about the header of the file was the same as Prince of Persia, except where the strings were to start nothing looked even relatively close to anything legible. I was never able to figure out the encryption, so I shelved that section of the project until a later date when I had more time to dedicate to it. Unfortunately, Ubisoft continues to make AC games. So for the most part my time gets spent figuring out the small differences in the forge file each time a new game is released.

Thanks for reading,
