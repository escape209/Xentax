Post by yaz0r » Mon Mar 14, 2011 10:49 pm

SoftIce wrote: And sorry if this is a totally retarded question, but do you search for them using a hex editor? I'd love to be able to help you out somehow.
It's a very long and convoluted process. I started with the list of files that are stored in the game exe, then the list of models that is stored in one of those files, brute-forcing some of the variations. I made a modified version of kh2 that would print the name all the files it loads, so I could add those to the list.
Nowadays, I use the fact that all files are stored in alphabetical order on the disc. So if I'm missing the name of one particular file, I can deduce part of its name from the name of the file before and after it, and brute-forcing the rest.
Sound files were brute-forced based on their header.

anm/eh_l/w_ex010/EEH506##L.anb
where ## = 13-16, 20-24
There are many more; just replace your anm/eh_r/w_ex010/EEH#####R.anb filenames with eh_l/w_ex010/EEH#####L.anb and add that to your table.
anm/tt_l/w_ex010/ETT###0#L.anb
where ###0# is 61303, 61304, 61402, 80804, 80805, & 80806.

where # = 2, 3, & 5
event/effect/di_event_#9#.pax
where #9# = 899, 997, and 999
event/effect/tr_event_101.pax
I found them by looking at the ARD files.
Also, your dumper doesn't cover apdx files because it's only for Final Mix. By renaming all the a.fm files in your name table to apdx, you get all the apdx files.

Post by Mirrorman95 » Mon Mar 21, 2011 1:42 am

sojakai wrote:How does one open the .wpn files or convert them to .obj?
At the present time, you can't. Yaz0r and Revelation are the only ones who have cracked the mdls format, so only they would have any idea as how to view wpn models. They look much like mdls files but without part of their header, and they may all use the same header because of that, so you could try taking the file headers of other, similar mdls files and use them for the wpns.

On another note, I have compared the models of the US and FM versions of KH2 and have a list of most of the models that were changed in Final Mix. However, the list is composed of all the files I have in one folder, so I may or may not be able to type them up. I had to check many of the files to see if they were changed only a fraction of a kilobyte in size.
