Post by erik945 » Sat Oct 31, 2020 10:59 pm

Use Noesis or another editor to convert textures to any common format (png, tif, tga).
The texture is fine. The color of the exposed parts of the body is correct, the color of the armor is determined by the mask. It looks like an ID0 texture.

Look at the skins. There are solid repaints. Keeping a different texture for everyone will take up a lot of space. And so you can store one mask and just change the color codes for each of the channels.

NorseInMyBlood wrote: ↑Thu Nov 05, 2020 5:36 pm This is probably a dumb question, but what is the tone map? Is it a texture that can be utilized or is it akin to the ID texture where it only serves a purpose in the game engine? Other than that I've also been struggling to apply the textures optimally in Blender, I feel like I'm missing something. Thanks in advance for any help.
Tone map basically adds extra detail to the model. The green channel can act as a specular map or even an overlay for the diffuse/color texture. the blue can be used for adding dirt/grime or gloss to certain parts of the clothing.
