Post by Ernegien » Mon Jan 10, 2011 7:31 pm

If I may say a few words...

I see that javascript files are getting gzipped before being sent across the wires, but that's about it. Could you possibly ask your hosting company to reconfigure Apache to also gzip php files for your site? It can easily be done and would save you (and them) quite a bit on bandwidth if they can spare the small increase in cpu useage. The prezipped files get cached anyways so this increase is quite minimal.

If they do not want to go this route (I don't see why they wouldn't), you could at least remove the whitespace from the html or css that's getting served, either directly in some of the phbb files or through some magical plugin if one exists. I'm not very familiar with phpbb, but there may be some useful caching/compression addons to help with the bandwidth. The other thing they could do that might help would be to add expires headers for static content that isn't very likely to change, that way browsers are more likely to cache it.

Having stricter limits on avatar and signature sizes may also help a bit, and force people to compress their images a bit more if they want them hosted on your servers. Someone also brought up a good point earlier, and it may be worthwhile to sift through your server logs and determine if any ddos attacks are contributing to this increase in bandwidth usage. There's also the option of splitting up the forum/blog/files hosting into separate servers/providers. Depending on the usage of each, it may be more beneficial to go that route instead...

Also, if you don't mind me asking, what is your current hosting setup and costs associated with it? I'd prefer if things were more efficient before donating, as the money would then become more useful that way...

Post by bradsmithee » Tue Jan 11, 2011 8:06 pm

I definitely would be against any type of ads since preservation of site integrity should be a common theme that separates you from other such outlets. Accepting donations yearly/bi-yearly and/or getting a cheaper (or donated) host are always better options for practical results.

Additionally, going "off the grid" just to minimize user-base and conserve bandwidth is generally NOT a good thing. I say this with conviction based on personal experience from my own half-hearted game development community forums. Yes, you would definitely see a decrease in overall traffic and thus users who just leech your content, but you'd also see a decrease in the ever-so-often intelligent researchers and developers that might frequent as regulars and help expand the core concepts I'm sure you started this site for to begin with. Besides that, if your bandwidth isn't largely coming from file downloads and more-so from script requests, I hardly think THOSE users are to blame since they probably just register, visit the site once, get a file, and then are gone forever.

If you are only tipping the scales in the 80GB bandwidth range, you shouldn't have an issue with a solid shared hosting package at a dozen locations. I use dreamhost personally and before they decided to tag themselves as "unlimited" bandwidth, my static number fell in the 2TB a month range -- of which I managed to tip a quarter of on a couple months. More importantly in the case of an active forums like this would be CPU usage (since it's a shared box). No, I cannot fully guarantee that you wouldn't use a gargantuan amount of CPU for your forums; however, I can say that I too run an old phpBB2 forum with a few thousand users and somewhat similar activity levels as this one without any hassle. It would certainly be worth a shot and a worthy investment to give them a try.

Then again, I AM one of the described people who basically was dropped a link for this site by a non-english-speaking 3D artist I hardly know in an effort to help finish up some code for conversion of an obscure file format. The only reason I'm really writing this post or even remember this forum is primarily because I found an email relating to donations being requested. While I only have $1.73 in my Paypal (which I could still donate if need be), I'm not against donating bandwidth, file hosting, or site backup/mirroring (which would probably be detrimental in your current state).

At any rate best of luck finding an overall solution.
